How the numbers work

1RM estimate. Uses the Epley formula (weight × (1 + reps ÷ 30)), with Brzycki shown as a cross-check. Both are most accurate at 5 reps or fewer. Past ~10 reps they drift and tend to overestimate — the confidence dot warns you. For sporadic training this matters: a heavy triple tells you far more than a light set of fifteen.

Percentiles & goals. Standards are approximate, drawn from aggregated public lifter data (Strength Level–style distributions) for adult males, keyed to bodyweight. Height is recorded for context (BMI) but isn't a percentile axis — leverages vary, and no reliable dataset segments lifts by height. Your goal for each lift is the weight that would place you at your chosen percentile, rounded to the nearest 2.5.

Weighted pull-ups. Computed on the full system load (bodyweight + added), then reported as the added weight. Enter only the weight you hang from the belt.

Reverse math. "At your weight → N reps" is the reps you'd need at the weight you just entered to prove your goal 1RM. "At your reps → W" is the weight that would prove it at the rep count you entered.
